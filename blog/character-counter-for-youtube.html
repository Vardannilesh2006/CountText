Character Counter for YouTube: Limits & Optimization Guide

YouTube is currently the second largest search engine in the world. With millions of hours of videos uploaded daily, optimizing metadata has become a critical step to ensure your content stands out, is indexed correctly, and gets clicked. Understanding the strict character limits of YouTube titles, descriptions, and tags is essential for success.

The Crucial YouTube Character Limits

Here is a breakdown of the character limits for different text sections on YouTube:

  • Video Title: Max 100 characters (Optimal: 50-70 characters).
  • Video Description: Max 5,000 characters.
  • Tags: Max 500 characters total across all tags.
  • Playlist Title: Max 150 characters.
  • Playlist Description: Max 5,000 characters.
  • YouTube Channel Name: Max 50 characters.
  • YouTube Channel Bio (About): Max 1,000 characters.

Why Video Title Optimization Matters

While YouTube allows up to 100 characters in titles, mobile devices and search engines (like Google and YouTube search listings) usually truncate titles after 50 to 70 characters. If you place your primary keywords or hook at the end of a 90-character title, viewers will not see it on their screen, leading to a much lower click-through rate (CTR).

Always use a character counter tool (such as the CountText dashboard) to verify that your title sits in the sweet spot of 50-70 characters. Place your target keywords at the absolute beginning of your title.

Making the Best Use of YouTube Descriptions (5,000 Characters)

The 5,000-character description box is a massive playground for search engines. However, only the first 150 characters of your description appear in search results snippets as a teaser, and only the first 2-3 lines appear "above the fold" before viewers have to click "Show More."

Use the first 150 characters of your description as a compelling hook summarizing the video and containing key search phrases. Use the remaining space for chapters/timestamps, website links, social links, and a detailed summary containing secondary keywords.

Character Counter for LinkedIn: Posts, Headlines & Profile Limits

LinkedIn is the top platform for business-to-business (B2B) marketing, professional networking, and personal branding. Unlike casual social apps, LinkedIn posts are often longer, more narrative, and structured. However, keeping tabs on character boundaries ensures your drafts retain their formatting and impact.

Key LinkedIn Profile & Post Limits

  • Post Updates: Max 3,000 characters.
  • Profile Headline: Max 220 characters.
  • About Summary Section: Max 2,600 characters.
  • Job Title: Max 100 characters.
  • Company Page Description: Max 2,000 characters.

Avoiding Post Truncation: The 'See More' Hook

While you have 3,000 characters for a single post update, the feed display only shows the first **140 to 200 characters** before placing a "...see more" link. If your opening hook does not capture the reader's attention instantly, they will scroll past. Use the first 2 lines to set up a question, paradox, or strong statement to drive clicks on the "see more" button.

Optimal Profile Headline (220 Characters)

Your headline is the most visible text on LinkedIn. It is shown next to your name in comment sections, search listings, and invitation requests. Instead of just writing your current job title, utilize the 220-character space to add your value proposition, core skills, or keywords that recruiters search for.

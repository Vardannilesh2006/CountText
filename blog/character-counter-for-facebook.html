Character Counter for Facebook: Posts, Ads & Page Limits

Facebook allows massive paragraphs in standard updates, but that doesn't mean writing long blocks is good for user engagement. In fact, advertising, page descriptions, and organic posts all perform better when they are short and direct. Let's study Facebook's copy guidelines.

Summary of Facebook Copy Limits

  • Standard Wall Post: 63,206 characters.
  • Page Short Bio (Description): Max 255 characters.
  • Facebook Ads Primary Text: Max 125 characters (before truncation).
  • Facebook Ads Headline: Max 40 characters.
  • Facebook Ads Link Description: Max 30 characters.

The 63,206 Character Myth

While Facebook theoretically allows a wall post to be up to **63,206 characters** long, standard posts that exceed **400 characters** are truncated behind a "See More" link. For organic business posts, the sweet spot for maximum engagement is between **40 to 80 characters**. Short posts prevent visual fatigue and drive direct interaction.

Facebook Ad Copy Optimization

When creating Facebook Ads, your Primary Text should be kept under **125 characters** to ensure it displays without truncation on mobile devices. Your Ad Headline must be kept under **40 characters** to prevent overlap with call-to-action buttons (such as "Learn More" or "Shop Now").

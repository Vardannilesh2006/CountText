Character Counter for X/Twitter: Post, Thread & Bio Limits

X (formerly Twitter) is a microblogging platform built on brief, fast-paced, and concise text exchange. Managing character space is the core element of drafting a viral tweet. Let's cover the essential limits of X today.

Summary of X/Twitter Text Limits

  • Standard Post (Free Accounts): 280 characters.
  • Premium Account Posts: Up to 25,000 characters.
  • Bio Section: Max 160 characters.
  • Username (Handle): Max 15 characters.
  • Display Name: Max 50 characters.
  • Direct Message (DM): Max 10,000 characters.

Understanding the 280-Character Rule

The standard post limit is **280 characters** for most accounts. However, it is crucial to remember that not all characters count equally in Twitter's API. For example, any link (URL) counts as exactly **23 characters**, regardless of its actual length. Emojis also count as 2 characters under specific Unicode rules.

Threads & Bio Limits

If your post exceeds 280 characters, you can either upgrade to X Premium or split your draft into a **Thread**. A thread keeps readers engaged, but it is vital to number your posts (e.g., "1/5", "2/5") to maintain flow. Additionally, your Profile Bio is strictly capped at **160 characters**, so keep your self-introduction punchy.
